Hi there...I'm Chidambara Nadig. I go by Chidu for short. Welcome to my personal webpage!
I currenly live in San Francisco, popularly called The City By the Bay!

I'm originally from Bangalore, India where I was born and brought up. I lived there until I finished under-grad school with a Bachelors of Engineering Degree in CSE.

I came to the US in 2012 to pursue a Master's Degree in CSE from The State University of New York at Buffalo (SUNY Buffalo).

I moved to San Francisco in Summer 2014 soon after graduating and even today, I still cant get over this city's incredible whether! I've been a Software Engineer at AOL Platforms since then, upto late August this year.

You can find me in the Mission District, rocking out to some guitar tunes at my guitar teacher's studio! Or maybe up on the ski slopes at Lake Tahoe if its the Winter season. Otherwise, I'm usually frequenting the tennis courts close to my house around Japantown and Hayes Valley!

I'm right now looking for opportunities to work in fun places in San Francisco. New York and Seattle are two other cities I love! If you are a recruiter and would like to hire me, scroll down below to see how you can get in touch with me! If you're not, still, ping me to say Hi!

Work

Software Engineer, AOL Platforms

June 2014 - August 2016

I specialized in Distributed Systems and Data Intensive Computing as a part of my Master's program at SUNY Buffalo. I'd taken a course on Data Intesive Computing, which kindled my passion for Pig scripts!

I started working as a Software Engineer at Millennial Media - a Mobile Ad Tech company - that got acquired by AOL in October 2015.

I worked with a team of brilliant engineers from whom I learnt a great deal, from architectural design of Large Scale Distributed applications to shipping quality code.

We worked exhaustively on the Data Management Platform and built several products that helped analyze and improve Ad Campaign Targeting.

I've mostly been working on Spark Applications with Scala for a year and half.

Check out my resume to find out more on what I've been upto at AOL.
